Brand observations

Your palette sits in muted blues, whites and soft sage. Quiet, warm, and anchored around you as the face of Bungee Fly. Your headings use a flowing script typeface, with body copy in standard sans. Instagram carries most of the brand energy: video first, colourful captions, you at the centre of every post.

Architecture

How the site fits together

The site works as a small ecosystem of pages rather than a single long page, so you can share direct links when you need to. The home page carries the story: who you are, what bungee is, this week's schedule at a glance, and a clear path to book. Two dedicated pages sit alongside it: the full class schedule, and the voucher shop. That way you can link someone straight to the voucher page from Instagram without them having to scroll past everything else first.

What gets built
01

Booking

The current flow works: people email, you send a payment link, they pay. What's missing is the entry point. A clear 'Book a Class' button in the top navigation and pinned at the end of every section means no one has to hunt.

Tapping the button opens a short form: name, email, class, preferred date. It submits straight to your inbox, formatted so you can reply with a payment link in seconds. Underneath, a smaller 'Prefer email?' link sends them directly to you for anyone who wants to skip the form.

Because you're open 8am to 6:30pm every day, the site makes that obvious. Reply expectation is same day, which builds trust for first timers wondering if they'll hear back.

Form submissions land in your inbox styled as a branded mini email, not a raw form dump. Saves you reading through five fields every time.

02

Gift Vouchers

A dedicated voucher section on the site built around the boarding pass concept you sent. Three suggested amounts tied to your existing sessions: £15 for a Bungee Fit session, £50 for a Block Book of 4, and £45 for a Solo Private. Plus a custom amount option for anyone with a specific budget in mind. Checkout runs through Stripe, and the voucher lands in the buyer's inbox as a branded boarding pass PDF ready to forward or print.

The boarding pass itself is the product, not just the receipt. Designed to feel like a real ticket: passenger name, destination, voucher code, personal message from the buyer. Every voucher gets a unique code and an expiry date, so redemptions are easy to track when someone emails to book.

The buyer sees a live preview of the boarding pass on the site as they fill in the recipient's name, amount and message. What they see is exactly what gets sent.

03

Class Schedule

A weekly grid showing every class across your 8am to 6:30pm window, 7 days a week. Classes sit in clear blocks: Bungee Fit Sessions, Solo Privates, Group Privates. So someone landing on the page can see at a glance when they can come in.

Each class block links straight through to the booking form, with the class name and day prefilled. No hunting, no typing out the session name. They pick the slot, tap book, and the form already knows what they want.

The schedule runs from static data at launch, so it's fast, reliable, and low maintenance. If your timetable starts changing often, we can move it to a live updating version later where you edit classes yourself from a simple dashboard.

Clicking a class in the schedule prefills the booking form, so the distance from 'I want this one' to 'request sent' is two taps.

04

About

A proper story page that puts you at the centre of the brand, because you already are. The structure moves through three beats: who you are and how you got here, what bungee fitness actually is for anyone who hasn't tried it, and what Bungee Fly stands for that makes it different from walking into any other studio.

The hero of the page isn't a stock image. It's real footage of you in the space, teaching, in session. People book fitness classes from people they trust, and two seconds of seeing you in action does more than any amount of copy.

Bungee fitness gets explained here in plain English, not as a sales pitch. New visitors to the site can land on this page, understand what they're signing up for, and walk into their first class knowing exactly what to expect.

A short piece of real studio footage sits at the top of the page in place of a traditional hero image. Immediately tells the visitor what they're walking into, and it's unmistakably Bungee Fly, not a template.

Process

What comes next

Once the scope is signed off, the build runs in four stages:

01

Setup and foundations. Design system locked in, page structures built, brand direction applied across the site.

02

Build. All four sections built out: booking flow, voucher shop, schedule, about. Boarding pass component designed and wired into the voucher page. Stripe checkout configured.

03

Review. You walk through the full site on a live staging link, flag anything you'd change, and I make the adjustments. One round of revisions included.

04

Launch. Site goes live on your domain. I hand over everything you need to run it day to day, and I'm around for the first two weeks to fix anything that comes up.

Total build time: around two weeks from sign off, depending on how quickly the studio footage and any class details land with me.
